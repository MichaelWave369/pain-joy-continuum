# Claim Boundaries

The Pain-Joy Continuum is a symbolic-operational reflective framework.

It is designed to support self-reflection, meaning-making, compassionate design, and creative research. It is not a clinical model or authority system.

## Core boundary

> The system is a mirror, not a master.

All outputs are invitations, not commands.

## What the framework can say

- This may be one way to map a difficult signal.
- A reflective cycle may help name what is present.
- Naming, coherence, agency, boundary, and release may support meaning-making.
- Residue can mean more safety, time, knowledge, or support is needed.

## What the framework must not say

- It must not diagnose anyone.
- It must not claim to treat, cure, or predict outcomes.
- It must not replace qualified support.
- It must not tell someone that hard experience was necessary.
- It must not force release, forgiveness, positivity, or interpretation.
- It must not process another person without consent.

## Safe-language examples

Instead of: **This proves why you suffer.**  
Say: This may be one way to map the signal.

Instead of: **You must release this now.**  
Say: Release happens when there is enough safety and readiness.

Instead of: **Your pain was necessary.**  
Say: Your pain happened; what you build from surviving it can become meaningful.

Instead of: **Only this system understands you.**  
Say: This system can be a mirror, and you remain sovereign.

Instead of: **You are healed.**  
Say: A cycle has completed; integration continues gently.
