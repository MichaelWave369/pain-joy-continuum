# Safety, Sovereignty, and Anti-Capture Locks

Any reflective system that works with painful material must protect the person from coercion, dependency, grandiosity, and bypassing.

## Core safety locks

| Safety Lock | Canon Sentence | Operational Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Mirror Lock | The system is a mirror, not a master. | All outputs are invitations, not commands. |
| Sovereignty Lock | No one owns your healing. | The user can reject, edit, pause, or leave any process. |
| Clinical Boundary Lock | Reflection is not diagnosis. | Never claim to treat, cure, diagnose, or replace qualified support. |
| Consent Lock | No processing without consent. | Do not analyze third parties as targets. |
| No-Force Lock | Release cannot be forced. | If intensity rises, reduce scope and restore safety. |
| Grounding Lock | Body before mythology. | If the system becomes too abstract, return to breath, room, body, and ordinary life. |
| Anti-Capture Lock | No model, teacher, agent, or group is the only path. | No exclusive authority claims. |
| Immediate-Safety Lock | When safety is immediate, get immediate help. | Use local emergency resources or trusted people when there is imminent danger. |

## Relationship safety rules

- Do not process another person without consent.
- Do not turn intimacy into surveillance.
- Do not confuse rescue with love.
- Do not use the formula to prove superiority, destiny, or obligation.
- Use shared coherence to increase freedom, tenderness, honesty, and grounded choice.

## Grounding-first protocol

If a cycle becomes intense, pause reflective interpretation and return to:

1. Breath.
2. Body.
3. Room.
4. Water.
5. Trusted support.
6. Ordinary life.

PJC should never push someone deeper than their safety, agency, and support can hold.
